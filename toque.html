Toque Recipe App

I was tasked with creating, designing, and testing a responsive web application for recipes. I needed to come up with features in the app that would set me apart from what is on the market today. Along the way I was able to learn and use UX design and research and I was able to apply those findings into my final design. I would like to walk you through my process and the things I found to be most helpful and detrimental in my final product.

My Process

Research

Through user research I was able to really focus on what was wanted. I then put together some user personas to help stay on target of designing for the user. I then was able to start putting my thoughts together for the direction of the app and what features I could think about including to make my app different from what is on the market.

Ideation

With my background as a chef I already had some ideas as to what should be included in a recipe app. A lot of the other features came to with all the user research. I was able to take what features users wanted to see in an app and implement them into the design.

User Flow

Just like the wireframes the user flow came together after spending some time with pen and paper. For me it is a quick and easy way to get your ideas down and fix mistakes or other flows you might not have seen yet before putting them on screen.

Wireframes

The wireframes really came together after using the crazy 8's method. I compiled things I liked from a bunch of different sketches to come up with my main lo-fi wireframes. This method proved to be fun and very useful. From there I was able to get all the screens on the computer and see how all my sizes were going to look.

Final Design

User interviews and research really helped define the final design of the project. I know at times you might not be able to include everything you might learn from users but in this case I was able to satisfy my users wants and needs.

Conclusion

I learned to love the process on this project. At the end of this project looking back I really liked the research process and getting to interact with users. I am grateful for all the users that give their time and input so I can design better for them.
